Hair Inspiration

When asking for a haircut, it is most common to split the head up into two. Most hairstyles for men will cut more off the sides and leave more hair at the top of the head. There are countless ways to cut one's top and sides. There are also various ways a barber may connect the top hair to the side hair. If your barber is willing, they may take care of your facial hair as well. Therefore, when giving your barber instructions be as precise as possible to ensure that you get your desired hairstyle. Each photo has intructions for clients asking their barber what they want and for barbers who want to learn how to replicate it. Below is a quick overview of common instructions and their meanings.

Instruction Translation
Fade Cutting technique that tapers the hair so it eventually “fades”. Most commonly, bald to 1/4 inch (#2).
Taper Gradual reduction in thickness towards one end.
Number "X" on the sides Leave "X" 1/8 inches of hair after cutting it. The higher the number, the less hair is cut. Most commonly the guards range from 0-8 with the 8 guard leaving 1 inch of hair.
Trim the top Take half an inch off the top
Line-up Make the edges of my hairline sharp
